Carrying the Fire: How God's Word Builds Unbreakable Faith and Unbreakable Families

Faith is a fire you carry — the truth and the Word of God, lighting your path and strengthening you for whatever lies ahead. Scripture has fed that fire for thousands of years, and it is available to you today.

Hearts Burning Within Us

After the resurrection, two disciples shared a meal with a stranger. Then he took bread, gave thanks, and broke it — and "their eyes were opened and they recognized him" (Luke 24:31). Some scholars suggest Jesus broke bread in a signature way the disciples knew. As he disappeared, they asked each other, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). Jesus taught the Word with such passion that hearts caught fire.

The Habit of Noble Hearts

The early church kept that fire burning: "Now the Berean Jews were of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11). Eagerness and daily examination — those marks of noble character can be yours, too.

The Weapon Jesus Chose

Tempted three times in the wilderness, Jesus answered every time by quoting Scripture. If using Scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me.

Fighting the enemy means fighting his lies, which mostly target how you see yourself and others:

  • You are hopeless; you can't grow; you can't overcome.
  • Other people will never grow and never change.
  • Heavy, hopeless thoughts are simply the way life is.

Every one of these is a lie. With the Holy Spirit and an eager study of the Word, anyone can be transformed and redeemed. Even one day of bad thinking can set fear-driven decisions in motion that follow you for years. The Word is how you fight back.

A Word for Every Corner of Life

The New Testament alone contains roughly a thousand commands for how to live: instructions on marriage and intimacy, a right relationship with alcohol, what a faithful minister should look like (1 and 2 Timothy), even envy and pride. Whatever your gifting — teacher, healer, prayer warrior — getting into the Word to strengthen you is never a bad idea. Its richness can make you better at love, forgiveness, service, your career, and every relationship you have.

The Armor That Ends with a Sword

"Put on the full armor of God, so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes" (Ephesians 6:11). The belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith that extinguishes the flaming arrows of the evil one — Paul's first readers knew these as heavy Roman battlefield gear. It all culminates here: "Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God" (Ephesians 6:17). Every piece of armor points to the Word.

When God's Voice Defies Common Sense

Should you marry this person? Move across the country? No amount of PhD research can answer questions like these. It's a God thing — and His instruction can contradict almost everything else you trust:

  • Your common sense
  • Your own desires
  • What your friends think and say
  • Even what your parents say — God is the heavenly parent with the true bird's-eye view

Yet His instruction will never contradict His written Word. That truth is eternal and unchanging, so the more you study Scripture, the sharper your discernment of God's voice grows.

The Lord Our God Is One

The foundational declaration of Judaism is the Shema: "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one" (Deuteronomy 6:4). Asked which of the six hundred-plus laws was greatest, Jesus opened with that very sentence, then continued: "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all your strength," and "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Mark 12:30-31). Love God, love people.

The Hebrew word for "one" here is echad — a oneness with complexity inside it. The same word appears when a man and his wife "become one flesh" (Genesis 2:24). The oneness of husband and wife shares the nature of the oneness of God, so if we want to see more of God in the world, we need to see more oneness in families.

Life Application

This week, take up the Berean habit: choose one passage — Ephesians 6:10-18 is a great start — and examine it every day with eagerness. Read it aloud, write down one truth each morning, and answer any lie that surfaces with the verse, just as Jesus did.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • When was the last time your heart burned within you over Scripture?
  • Which of the enemy's lies about yourself or others have you been quietly believing?
  • Where might God's instruction be contradicting your common sense or desires right now?
  • What would change in your closest relationships if you pursued the oneness Scripture describes?

You don't need to be a scholar to carry the fire — just pick it up. Open the Word with eagerness, let it arm you against every lie, and watch God build unbreakable faith and unbreakable families. If it's good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me.
