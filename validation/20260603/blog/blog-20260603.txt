Carrying the Fire: How God's Word Strengthens Your Faith and Builds Unbreakable Families

In Cormac McCarthy's novels, the survivors of a dark world describe themselves as "carrying the fire." For a follower of Jesus, the fire is the truth and the Word of God — carry it and it will light your path and strengthen you, as it has for believers across thousands of years.

What Made the Disciples' Hearts Burn?

After the resurrection, two disciples walked the road to Emmaus with a stranger and sat down to eat with him. When he took the bread, gave thanks, and broke it, their eyes were opened, perhaps because Jesus had his own unmistakable way of breaking bread. "They asked each other, 'Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?'" (Luke 24:32).

It was like the afterglow of a powerful worship concert — and that same fire is available to you.

Why Examine the Scriptures Every Day?

Luke writes that the Berean believers "were of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11). Noble character and daily time in the Word went hand in hand.

If It's Good Enough for Jesus, It's Good Enough for Me

Jesus met all three of the devil's wilderness temptations the same way: "It is written" (Matthew 4:1-11). If using Scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me.

Fighting the enemy means, above all, fighting his lies about you and the people around you. He wants you to believe:

- That you are hopeless, unable to grow, change, or overcome.
- That other people will never grow or change either.
- That heavy, defeated thinking is simply realistic.

Every one of these is a lie. With the Holy Spirit and an eager study of the Word, anyone can be transformed and redeemed. The lies attack daily. Even one day of choices made from fear, anxiety, pride, or insecurity can set a course you live with for years.

How Much of Life Does the Bible Speak To?

The New Testament alone contains roughly a thousand commands for living: instructions on marriage and intimacy, a right relationship with alcohol, what a minister should be (1 and 2 Timothy), how to handle envy and pride. Whatever your gifting (teaching, healing, prayer), the Word can make you better at love, forgiveness, service, your career, and every relationship you have.

Why Does the Armor of God End with a Sword?

In Ephesians 6:10-18, Paul urges you to "put on the full armor of God, so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes," for "our struggle is not against flesh and blood" but against spiritual forces of evil. He names the gear: the belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith that extinguishes the flaming arrows of the evil one, the helmet of salvation. The whole passage culminates in a single weapon: "the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God" (Ephesians 6:17).

How Do You Know When It's a God Thing?

Some decisions are too big for data, because no amount of PhD research can tell you whom to marry or where to move. It's a God thing, and his instruction can contradict the voices you trust most:

- Your common sense.
- Your own desires.
- What your friends say.
- Even what your parents say (honor them, but God has the real bird's-eye view).

Yet God's instruction will never contradict God's Word. His truth is eternal and unchanging, so the more you study the Scriptures, the sharper your discernment becomes.

The Lord Our God Is One

Asked which commandment is greatest, Jesus opened his answer with the Shema, the foundational declaration of Judaism: "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one" (Deuteronomy 6:4). Then he continued: love God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and "love your neighbor as yourself" (Mark 12:28-31). Love God, love people.

The Hebrew word for "one" is echad, describing not a bare singularity but a complex, united oneness. The same word appears in Genesis 2:24, where a man is united to his wife "and they become one flesh." The oneness of husband and wife is the very oneness of God, so if you want more of God in the world, long for more oneness in families. Jesus promised, "whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven" (Matthew 16:19) — what you build here is bound there.

Life Application

This week, practice being a Berean. Each day, read one short passage with eagerness, write down a verse that stands out, and carry it with you. When a lie surfaces about yourself or someone else, answer it the way Jesus did in the wilderness: with the written Word.

Ask yourself these questions:

- What lie about yourself or others have you been believing, and what does Scripture say instead?
- When has God led you to do something that made no sense to anyone else, and did it line up with His Word?
- Which of the Word's instructions (on marriage, envy, pride, or work) do you most need to study right now?
- Who could examine the Scriptures alongside you this week, so two hearts burn instead of one?

You don't need to be a scholar to begin, only eager. Open the Word daily, let it power you up, and keep carrying the fire — that is how God builds unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.
