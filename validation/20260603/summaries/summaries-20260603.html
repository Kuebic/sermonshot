Long

The pastor opens his new series, Carrying the Fire, by sharing his passion for the Bible as a source of spiritual strength. The series takes its name from a phrase by Cormac McCarthy; the fire is the truth and the Word, and carrying it lights your path. He begins on the road to Emmaus, where two disciples recognized the resurrected Jesus as he broke bread and asked each other whether their hearts had been burning while he opened the Scriptures to them (Luke 24:30-32). That same hunger marked the Bereans, who received the message with eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day (Acts 17:11), and Jesus himself, who answered each of the devil's three temptations in the wilderness with Scripture (Matthew 4:1-11). The pastor's takeaway is simple: if it was good enough for Jesus, it is good enough for him.

Fighting the enemy, he explains, means fighting his lies — that you are hopeless and cannot grow, that other people will never change. With the Holy Spirit and an eager study of the Word, anybody can be transformed. The stakes are real: even one day of bad thinking can set a long course of decisions driven by fear and anxiety. Against that, the New Testament offers rich practical instruction — on marriage, alcohol, ministry, envy, and pride — and Paul's Armor of God passage (Ephesians 6:10-18) gathers it into one picture that culminates in the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God.

The biggest decisions in life — whom to marry, where to move — are a God thing, and God's instruction can contradict common sense, desires, friends, even parents, yet it will never contradict His Word; studying Scripture sharpens our discernment of His daily leading. The pastor closes with the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4), which Jesus quoted to open the greatest commandment (Mark 12:28-31): love God, love people. The Hebrew word echad reveals that the oneness of God is the same oneness as man and wife (Genesis 2:24), and what is bound on earth is bound in heaven (Matthew 16:19). The series aims at unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.
