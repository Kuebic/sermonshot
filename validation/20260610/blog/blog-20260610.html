Carry the Fire: Why All Spirituality Exists to Strengthen Your Love

From mountaintop meditation to morning prayer, the world is full of spiritual practices. But what is all that spirituality actually for? Scripture gives a surprisingly focused answer: God is spirit, and God is love — and every genuine spiritual pursuit exists to increase your strength to love. Grow spiritually and your capacity to love grows; stretch your love and you grow spiritually.

What Does It Mean That God Is Both Spirit and Love?

Two themes run through the whole Bible. From the very first page, God is spirit: "the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters" (Genesis 1:2). And in the New Testament, God is love: "Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love" (1 John 4:8). Put together: if your spirituality isn't warming up your heart, it isn't really serving you.

Jesus' parable of the sheep and the goats makes this concrete (Matthew 25:31-46). People are welcomed into the kingdom and can't figure out why, until the King explains: when you fed the hungry, clothed the naked, and visited the prisoner, you did it for me. Every act of love toward any person grows you spiritually, whether you realize who you're serving or not.

What Really Died in the Garden?

God warned Adam and Eve that eating the forbidden fruit meant certain death. The serpent insisted they wouldn't die. Strangely, both were true — they were using different definitions of death. God meant spiritual death: a hardened heart, disconnected from His life and His way of loving. Paul describes that condition exactly: "They are darkened in their understanding and separated from the life of God because of the ignorance that is in them due to the hardening of their hearts" (Ephesians 4:18).

So separation from God is a heart problem, and the remedy is simple to name: soften the heart. God promises exactly that: "I will remove from you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh" (Ezekiel 36:26).

How Does Satan Twist the Truth?

Here's a tip worth remembering: Satan loves to lie using nothing but truths. Like a tricky lawyer, the serpent told Eve she wouldn't die physically — technically accurate. The deception came in the call to action: so go ahead and eat the fruit. The same pattern runs through the world today — true fact after true fact, then a twisted conclusion. Watch the call to action. That's where the twist hides.

Why Can't Shame Reconnect You to God?

After the fall, Adam and Eve could still hear God; He came looking for an open-hearted conversation. Instead, Adam hid behind fig leaves, blamed Eve, and deflected. Our instinct when we fail runs the same way — feel more shame, stay in it longer, as if shame could pay the debt. But no amount of shame could reconnect them to God, because reconnection to God is the reconnection of the heart. Shame is not part of God's restoration plan. Ever since Genesis 3:24, the whole Bible is a story of reconnection — God warming our hearts back to His.

What Makes a Spiritual Gift Worth Anything?

Paul settles the question in one of the Bible's most beautiful passages: "If I speak in the tongues of men or of angels, but do not have love, I am only a resounding gong or a clanging cymbal... if I have a faith that can move mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing" (1 Corinthians 13:1-2). These aren't wimpy spiritual credentials — yet without love, they gain you nothing.

None of this argues against prayer, fasting, or faith. God uses religion to lift our eyes above earthly desires; the trouble starts when people bottle the prophets' fire and sell it. The practices matter — when they aim at love.

What Does "Eternal Life" Really Mean?

Asked to name the greatest commandment, Jesus boiled everything down to two things: "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all your strength... Love your neighbor as yourself. There is no commandment greater than these" (Mark 12:29-31). And he promised, "I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish" (John 10:27-28). Eternal life carries the sense of harmony with God — the oneness of two becoming one (Genesis 2:24). God created us to be His counterpart, children who would build the kingdom of heaven on earth. Love unconditionally the way Jesus loved, and you step into that harmony now.

Life Application

This week, go hunting for your forgiveness blind spots. It's easy to forgive the worker who gets your order wrong; it's much harder to forgive people in authority — the officer who wrote the ticket, the boss, the church leader, the government official. Pray about it, choose one person you've quietly held something against, and take one concrete step toward them in Jesus' pattern: forgive, serve, do the will of God.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Is my spiritual life actually increasing my strength to love, or has it become routine?
  • Where has my heart hardened — and what would softening look like this week?
  • When I fail, do I sit in shame, or do I bring my fears and pain to God honestly?
  • Whose voice am I trusting, and have I examined its call to action?

The Emmaus disciples asked, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). That burning is the whole point — a spark that grows your love for God and people. So this week, carry the fire.
