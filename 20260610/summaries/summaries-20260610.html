Long

In a world filled with countless spiritual practices and religious traditions, the true essence of spirituality often gets lost in complexity. However, Scripture reveals a profound truth: all genuine spirituality aims toward one goal - increasing our capacity to love. This connection between spirit and love isn't coincidental but fundamental to understanding God's nature and our relationship with Him.

God is revealed in Scripture as both spirit and love, and these aren't separate characteristics but intimately connected aspects of His being. The more we grow spiritually, the more capacity we develop to love others. Conversely, working on our ability to love actually constitutes spiritual growth. Our separation from God occurs not primarily through intellectual problems but through the hardening of our hearts, as described in Ephesians 4:17. When hearts become hardened, we disconnect from God's life force, but God promises transformation by replacing our heart of stone with a heart of flesh.

The story of Adam and Eve illustrates how spiritual death occurs through heart hardening rather than mere disobedience. Their real separation from God happened when they chose blame and shame-covering over honest, open-hearted relationship. This reveals an important truth: shame never reconnects us to God but actually hardens our hearts further. Even the most impressive spiritual gifts - speaking with angels, prophetic abilities, mountain-moving faith - are worthless without love, as Paul emphasizes in 1 Corinthians 13. Jesus simplified all spirituality into two essential commandments: love God with everything you have and love your neighbor as yourself. True spiritual growth happens when we identify our blind spots in loving others and work to soften our hearts toward those we struggle to forgive.
