Carry the Fire: Why All Spirituality Exists to Strengthen Your Love

Scripture makes two sweeping claims about who God is: God is spirit, and God is love. Held together, those truths reveal the purpose of your whole spiritual life. Every prayer, every practice, every act of faith is meant to do one thing — warm your heart and grow your strength to love.

God Is Spirit, and God Is Love

The Bible opens with God as spirit: "the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters" (Genesis 1:2). The New Testament adds, "Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love" (1 John 4:8). These threads belong together: spiritual growth and the capacity to love rise in step with each other. Among the world's countless spiritual practices, God's question stays simple. Is your heart warming up? Are you reconnecting to him on a love wavelength?

Separation from God Is a Hardening of the Heart

Paul describes people "darkened in their understanding and separated from the life of God because of the ignorance that is in them due to the hardening of their hearts" (Ephesians 4:18). The separation lives in the heart, so the way back is simple to name: soften it. God promises, "I will remove from you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh" (Ezekiel 36:26). A soft, warm, beating heart, full of love.

Every Act of Love Grows Your Spirit

Jesus tells a parable about people welcomed into the kingdom who cannot figure out why (Matthew 25:31-46). When they protest that they never fed, clothed, or visited him, the King answers, "whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me" (Matthew 25:40). Loving any person counts: whether or not you know who you are really serving, the act itself grows your spirit.

The Fall Was a Spiritual Death

God warned Adam and Eve that eating the forbidden fruit meant they would surely die. The serpent insisted they would not. Both were right — they meant different deaths. God meant spiritual death: a hardened heart, cut off from his life force. Satan, like a tricky lawyer, meant physical death. Satan loves it when he can lie to you using nothing but truths; he recites accurate facts, then twists the call to action — you will not drop dead, so go ahead and eat. In everything the world tells you, watch the call to action.

Shame Will Never Reconnect You to God

Even after they ate, Adam and Eve could still hear God. He came looking for an open-hearted conversation — "Where are you?" — hoping Adam would bring his fear and pain honestly. Instead, Adam hid behind fig leaves and shifted the blame. No amount of shame could have reconnected them to God, because reconnection means the heart learning to love like God again. Instinct says to sit in shame longer, as if it were payment, but shame has never been part of God's restoration plan. Ever since Genesis 3:24, the whole story of the Bible has been a story of reconnection.

Without Love, Even Mountain-Moving Faith Gains Nothing

Does that make prayer, fasting, and faith useless? Hardly — though God grieves what we do with religion: prophets carry the fire to set hearts ablaze, then people bottle it, control it, and sell it. Paul settles the priority: "If I speak in the tongues of men or of angels, but do not have love, I am only a resounding gong or a clanging cymbal" (1 Corinthians 13:1). Prophecy, mountain-moving faith, giving away everything — without love, they gain nothing (1 Corinthians 13:2-3).

Eternal Life Is Harmony with God

Asked for the greatest commandment, Jesus answered, "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all your strength... Love your neighbor as yourself" (Mark 12:29-31). Boil down every ordinance and you get two things: love God and love people. Jesus promised, "I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish" (John 10:28) — and eternal life carries the sense of harmony with God. Even sinless Adam and Eve were given a kingdom to build for him. Forgive, serve, do God's will — love unconditionally as Jesus loved — and you step into that harmony.

Life Application

This week, go hunting for your forgiveness blind spots. It is easy to forgive a fumbled order at the drive-through; it is much harder to forgive people in authority — the officer who writes the ticket, the boss, the church leader, the government official. Each day, ask God to show you one blind spot, then deliberately release one person you have been holding something against.

Ask yourself these questions:

- Where has your heart quietly hardened, and what would softening it look like?
- Who is hardest for you to forgive, and what does that reveal about your blind spots?
- When you fail, do you sit in shame, or bring your whole heart honestly to God?
- What is one act of love or service you can offer "the least of these" this week?

God is spirit, and God is love — and all spiritual pursuits are ultimately for the strengthening of our love. Let your heart burn like the disciples' on the Emmaus road: "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). Warm your heart, love unconditionally, and carry the fire.
