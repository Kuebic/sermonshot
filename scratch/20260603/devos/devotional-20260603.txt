5 Day Devotional

Day 1: Hearts Burning Within

Devotional
Two discouraged disciples once walked a long road with a stranger who explained the Scriptures as they went. They didn't recognize Jesus until he sat at their table, took the bread, gave thanks, and broke it. Some scholars believe Jesus had a signature style of breaking bread, and something about the way he divided it opened their eyes. They recognized him, and he disappeared from their sight.

Afterward they talked first about what had happened inside them while he taught: were not our hearts burning within us while he opened the Scriptures to us? The Word, opened up by Jesus, left them in awe, like walking out of an awesome worship concert with chills and a wave of inspiration you can't shake.

That same fire is meant for you. If your time in Scripture has gone cold, a duty to check off rather than an encounter to anticipate, remember that Jesus taught a passion for the Word. He still opens the Scriptures to those who walk with him. Come to the Bible today expecting to meet him there, and don't be surprised if your heart starts burning.

Bible Verse
'They asked each other, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?"' - Luke 24:32

Reflection Question
When did your heart last burn as you read Scripture, and what would it take for you to come to the Word expecting to encounter Jesus there?

Quote
"The disciples were just in awe. Something was burning inside them."

Prayer
Jesus, open the Scriptures to me the way you opened them on the road, and set my heart burning with passion for your Word. Amen.

Day 2: Examine It Daily

Devotional
When Luke wrote the book of Acts, he paused to single out one church for special praise. The believers in Berea, he said, were more noble in character than those in Thessalonica. His reason: they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day.

Luke ties their nobility to a habit rather than to wealth, education, or status. The noble ones were the eager ones: believers who took what they heard in preaching and opened the Scriptures for themselves, day after day, to see if it was true. In the early churches, better character grew out of daily, eager examination of the Word.

That connection still holds for you. Quiet daily moments form character, more than any single dramatic one. The New Testament alone holds a thousand commands for how to live: instructions on marriage, envy, pride, ministry, and relationships of many kinds. There is so much richness in the Word, but it shapes you only when you get into it. Don't settle for secondhand faith. Take what you hear, even from a pastor you trust, back to the Book itself. Examine it with eagerness, every day, and let the Scriptures do their slow, noble work in you.

Bible Verse
'Now the Berean Jews were of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day to see if what Paul said was true.' - Acts 17:11

Reflection Question
What would change in your week if you examined the Scriptures every day with the eagerness of the Bereans?

Quote
"So the disciples that were in the early churches, the ones that were of noble character, noticeably a better character, were eagerly examining the Scriptures every day."

Prayer
Lord, give me the eagerness of the Bereans, so that examining your Word becomes the daily habit that shapes my character. Amen.

Day 3: Fighting the Lies

Devotional
The enemy's primary weapon is deception. He lies, and he twists the truth, and a lot of those lies have to do with you. He wants you to see yourself as hopeless, unable to grow, unable to overcome. He wants you to see others as people who will never change. He wants everybody bogged down by hopeless, heavy thoughts. Even one day of bad thinking can do damage: decisions made out of fear, anxiety, pride, or insecurity can set you on a long course with ramifications that last for years.

Jesus shows you how to fight back. When the devil came and tempted him three times in the wilderness, Jesus responded the same way each time: he quoted Scripture. He beat back the enemy with the written Word of God. If using Scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for you.

The enemy works hard to keep one truth from you: with the Holy Spirit, with Jesus, and with an eagerness for the Word, anybody can be transformed and anybody can be redeemed, including you, and including the person you've given up on. The lies come on a daily basis, so arm yourself daily. Find the verse that answers the lie, and speak it.

Bible Verse
'Jesus answered, "It is written: Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God."' - Matthew 4:4

Reflection Question
What lie about yourself or someone else have you been treating as truth, and which verse will you use to fight back against it?

Quote
"When I thought about this today, I thought, hey, if using scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me."

Prayer
Father, when the enemy lies to me about myself or about others, put your Word in my mouth; if it was good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me. Amen.

Day 4: Power Up

Devotional
Each morning you put on clothes, shoes, maybe a watch. Paul tells you to put on armor too. When he told the Ephesians to put on the full armor of God, his readers knew what Roman armor looked like: heavy gear, iron and spikes, big-time equipment built for real battle. The belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, feet fitted with the readiness of the gospel of peace, the shield of faith that extinguishes the flaming arrows of the evil one, the helmet of salvation, the sword of the Spirit. All of that imagery culminates in one thing: the word of God.

Paul names the reason you need it: your struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the spiritual forces of evil. When the day of evil comes, you want to stand your ground, and after you have done everything, to stand firm.

Power up with the Word. Studying it is no egghead intellectual exercise; it gives you spiritual and emotional strength to be better at everything you want to be better at: better at love, better at forgiveness, better at service, better at your career and your relationships. As you study, your discernment sharpens too. God's instruction may contradict your common sense, your desires, even your friends' advice, but it will never contradict his Word.

Bible Verse
'Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God.' - Ephesians 6:17

Reflection Question
Which piece of the armor of God do you most often leave off, and what would it look like to take up the sword of the Spirit, the Word, before facing today?

Quote
"I want you to take this as spiritual, emotional power to be greater and to do more and to be better at everything you want to be better at. Better at love, better at forgiveness, better at service, better at your careers, better at any kind of relationship. Power you up with the Word."

Prayer
Lord, dress me in your full armor today, and let your Word be the sword in my hand and the strength in my heart. Amen.

Day 5: The Lord Is One

Devotional
The experts in the law tried to trap Jesus by asking him to name the most important commandment out of the Ten plus more than six hundred added ordinances. He began his answer with the Shema, the foundational declaration of Judaism: hear, O Israel, the Lord our God, the Lord is one. Then he continued: love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and love your neighbor as yourself. Love God, love people: vertical and horizontal.

There's a discovery buried in the Hebrew. The word for "one" in Deuteronomy 6:4 is echad, a oneness with complexity rather than the bare singular of having one phone. The other place you find echad is Genesis 2:24, where a man is united to his wife and the two become one flesh. The oneness of husband and wife is the very same word as the oneness of God. If you want to see more of God in the world, look for more oneness in families.

Jesus handed us the key: what is bound on earth is bound in heaven. Whether you're married or single, pursue oneness, with God first, and with the people he has joined to your life. Strive toward an unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.

Bible Verse
'Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one.' - Deuteronomy 6:4

Reflection Question
Where is God inviting you to pursue deeper oneness, with him and with the people closest to you?

Quote
"If we want to see more God in the world, we need to see more oneness of families in the world. That's that simple."

Prayer
Lord our God, you are one; make me one with you, and build in me an unbreakable faith and an unbreakable family. Amen.
