Summary

Kicking off a new midweek Bible series called "Carrying the Fire," the pastor shared his passion for the Bible as an incredible source of spiritual strength. He began on the road to Emmaus, where the resurrected Jesus broke bread with the disciples, their eyes were opened, and they asked, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). That burning heart for the Word, the pastor said, is what this series is after. He pointed to the Bereans, who "received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11), and to Jesus in the wilderness, who answered every temptation of the devil by quoting Scripture — "if it's good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me." The Word is how we fight the enemy's lies that we are hopeless, that we cannot grow, and that others will never change. The pastor walked through the Armor of God in Ephesians 6:10-18, noting that all the vivid imagery — belt, breastplate, shield, helmet — culminates in the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God.

The pastor then turned to how Scripture shapes our biggest life decisions. God's instruction can contradict our common sense, our desires, even what friends and parents say — but it will never contradict God's Word, because His truth is eternal and unchanging, and studying it strengthens our discernment. At the heart of the series is a central Hebrew discovery: Deuteronomy 6:4, the Shema — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one" — the foundational declaration of Judaism, which Jesus himself quoted when asked for the greatest commandment before saying, love God and love your neighbor (Mark 12:29-31). The Hebrew word for "one," echad, is not a simple singular oneness; it appears again in Genesis 2:24, where man and wife "become one flesh." The oneness of husband and wife shares the very nature of the oneness of God — so if we want to see more of God in the world, we need to see more oneness in families. The pastor closed with his hope for the series: that studying the Word would embolden everyone toward unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.

Intro Prayer

Heavenly Father, thank You for gathering us together around Your Word tonight. You have given us the Scriptures as a source of spiritual strength for thousands of years, and we ask that as we open them now, our hearts would burn within us just as the disciples' hearts burned on the road to Emmaus. Open our eyes to recognize Jesus, give us the eagerness of the Bereans to examine the Scriptures, and let Your truth light our path and strengthen us for everything You are calling us to. Guide our conversation, soften our hearts toward one another, and make us bold and honest as we share. In Jesus' name, Amen.

Questions

  • The pastor opened with the disciples on the road to Emmaus, who recognized Jesus when he broke the bread and asked, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). He compared it to the chills you feel after an awesome worship concert. When has the Bible, a sermon, or a worship moment left your heart "burning" like that?
  • The pastor pointed to the Bereans, who were "of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11). How would you honestly describe your own relationship with Scripture right now — eager, occasional, dutiful, distant? What draws you in or holds you back?
  • When the devil tempted Jesus three times in the wilderness, Jesus answered every time by quoting Scripture (Matthew 4:1-11). The pastor said, "If using Scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me." Have you ever used a specific verse or truth from the Bible to push back against temptation or a dark moment? What happened?
  • The pastor said the enemy's lies "have a lot to do with you and how you see yourself and how you see others" — that you are hopeless and can't grow, or that others will never change. Which of these lies do you find yourself believing most easily, and about whom?
  • The pastor admitted that he has made decisions "out of fear and anxiety" that set him on a long course with lasting ramifications, and that even one day of really bad thinking can lead to a lot of problems. Can you share a time when fear, anxiety, pride, or insecurity drove a decision in your life? How might Scripture have spoken into that moment?
  • Reading the Armor of God in Ephesians 6:10-18, the pastor noted that all the heavy imagery — the belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation — culminates in "the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God." Which piece of that armor feels most missing from your life right now, and why?
  • The pastor said God's instruction can contradict your common sense, your desires, your friends, and even your parents — but it will never contradict God's Word. Have you ever sensed God leading you to do something that made no sense to anyone else, even to yourself? How did you discern it was really Him?
  • The pastor's central Hebrew discovery was that the word for "one" in "the LORD our God, the LORD is one" (Deuteronomy 6:4) — echad — is the same word used when man and wife "become one flesh" (Genesis 2:24), and that if we want to see more of God in the world, we need to see more oneness in families. Where in your closest relationships — marriage, family, or this group — is God inviting you to pursue deeper oneness, and what would that honestly require of you?

Life Application

This week, take up the practice of the Bereans: examine the Scriptures every day with eagerness. Each day, read a portion of the Armor of God passage (Ephesians 6:10-18) and choose one piece of armor — truth, righteousness, peace, faith, salvation, or the word of God — to consciously "put on" that day, especially when you notice the enemy's lies about yourself or others creeping in. Jot down one sentence each day about what you noticed. At the next group meeting, share which piece of armor you needed most this week and one moment where the Word actually strengthened you.

Key Takeaways

  • The Bible is an incredible source of spiritual strength, and encountering Jesus in the Scriptures can leave our hearts burning within us like the disciples on the road to Emmaus.
  • Jesus fought the devil's temptations in the wilderness by quoting Scripture, and we can use the Word the same way to fight the enemy's lies that we are hopeless and that others can never change.
  • The Armor of God in Ephesians 6:10-18 culminates in the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God.
  • God's instruction may contradict our common sense, our desires, and the advice of friends and parents, but it will never contradict His Word, because His truth is eternal and unchanging.
  • The Hebrew word echad reveals that the oneness of God (Deuteronomy 6:4) and the oneness of husband and wife (Genesis 2:24) share the same nature, so studying the Word emboldens us toward unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.

Make us like the Bereans, eager to examine the Scriptures every day, and clothe us in Your full armor — the belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit — so we can stand firm against every lie of the enemy that says we are hopeless or that others cannot change.
