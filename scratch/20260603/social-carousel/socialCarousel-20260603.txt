Carousel 1: Hearts Burning for the Word

Title : "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" — Luke 24:32 (NIV)

- Slide 1: After the cross, two disciples shared a table with someone they didn't recognize — until he broke the bread.
- Slide 2: In that moment their eyes were opened, and they realized the risen Jesus had been opening the Scriptures to them the whole time.
- Slide 3: Their first words weren't about the miracle — they were about the fire: "Were not our hearts burning within us?"
- Slide 4: That same passion marked the early church, where the Bereans were called noble in character because they "examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11).
- Slide 5: When you come to the Word with eagerness, it's not an intellectual exercise — it's spiritual and emotional power to be better at love, forgiveness, service, and every relationship you have.
- Slide 6: Carry the fire: the Word can light your path and strengthen you, just like it has for thousands of years.

Carousel 2: Fighting the Enemy's Lies

Title : "If using scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me."

- Slide 1: When the devil tempted Jesus three times in the wilderness, Jesus answered every single time by quoting Scripture.
- Slide 2: The enemy's primary weapon is lies — he wants you to see yourself as hopeless, and to see others as people who will never grow or change.
- Slide 3: Even one day of really bad thinking can send you down a long course of decisions made out of fear, anxiety, pride, or insecurity.
- Slide 4: But Paul says we have armor for this fight: the belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation — and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God (Ephesians 6:10-18).
- Slide 5: With the Holy Spirit and an eagerness for the Word, anybody can be transformed and anybody can be redeemed — that's the real truth that extinguishes every flaming arrow.
- Slide 6: Put on the full armor of God, and when the day of evil comes, you will be able to stand your ground.

Carousel 3: The Oneness of God

Title : "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one." — Deuteronomy 6:4 (NIV)

- Slide 1: When Jesus was asked to name the most important commandment out of the Ten Commandments and 600-plus added ordinances, he didn't start with a rule — he started with the Shema: "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one."
- Slide 2: Then he gave the greatest commandments: love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and love your neighbor as yourself (Mark 12:29-31).
- Slide 3: The Hebrew word for "one" is echad — not a singular oneness like one phone, but a oneness with complexity.
- Slide 4: The same word appears in Genesis 2:24, where a man is united to his wife and the two become one flesh — the oneness of husband and wife shares the very nature of the oneness of God.
- Slide 5: So if we want to see more of God in the world, we need to see more oneness in families — because what is bound on earth is bound in heaven (Matthew 16:19).
- Slide 6: Study the Word with eagerness, and let it embolden you toward unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.
