Long

The pastor opens a new Wednesday Bible series, "Carrying the Fire" — a phrase borrowed from Cormac McCarthy — where the fire is the truth of God's Word, carried to light our path and strengthen us. The inspiration comes from the disciples on the road to Emmaus, who recognized the resurrected Jesus in the breaking of the bread and asked, "Were not our hearts burning within us while he opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). That same burning passion marked the early church: the Bereans were called noble in character because they received the message with eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day (Acts 17:11).

Scripture is not just for knowing — it is for fighting. When the devil tempted Jesus in the wilderness, Jesus answered every temptation by quoting Scripture, and the pastor's refrain follows: if it's good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me. The enemy's primary weapon is lies — that you are hopeless, that you can't grow, that others will never change — but with the Holy Spirit and an eagerness for the Word, anybody can be transformed. The Armor of God (Ephesians 6:10-18) pictures this battle vividly: the belt of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation, all culminating in the sword of the Spirit, which is the Word of God. Studying Scripture also sharpens discernment, because while God's instruction may contradict common sense, desires, or even family advice, it will never contradict His Word.

The series' central discovery lies in the Shema, Judaism's foundational declaration: "Hear, O Israel: the Lord our God, the Lord is one" (Deuteronomy 6:4) — the very words Jesus used to open his answer about the greatest commandment: love God, love people. The Hebrew word for one, echad, signifies a complex oneness, the same word used when husband and wife become one flesh (Genesis 2:24). The oneness of marriage reflects the oneness of God, and what is bound on earth is bound in heaven. The goal: unbreakable faith and unbreakable families.
