Carrying the Fire: How a Passion for Scripture Can Strengthen Your Faith and Build an Unbreakable Life

The Bible is not an egghead intellectual exercise. It is fire — truth and word that light your path and strengthen you, with power to make you better at love, forgiveness, service, your career, every relationship.

Hearts Burning Within Us

After the resurrection, two disciples walked with Jesus without recognizing him — until "he took bread, gave thanks, broke it and began to give it to them. Then their eyes were opened and they recognized him" (Luke 24:30-31). Some scholars believe Jesus had a signature way of breaking bread.

Their response: "Were not our hearts burning within us while he talked with us on the road and opened the Scriptures to us?" (Luke 24:32). Like leaving an awesome worship concert — chills, a wave of inspiration. Jesus taught with a passion that set hearts on fire, and that fire is for you.

Examine the Scriptures Every Day

The Bereans "were of more noble character than those in Thessalonica, for they received the message with great eagerness and examined the Scriptures every day" (Acts 17:11). Noble character and a daily appetite for the Word go hand in hand.

Your Weapon Against the Enemy's Lies

When the devil tempted him in the wilderness, Jesus answered all three temptations with Scripture (Matthew 4:1-11). If using Scripture to beat back the enemy is good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me.

Fighting the enemy means fighting his lies about how you see yourself and others. He wants you to believe:

  • You are hopeless — you can't grow and can't overcome.
  • Other people will never grow and will never change.
  • Everyone should stay bogged down by hopeless, heavy thoughts.

These are all lies. With the Holy Spirit, with Jesus, and with an eagerness for the Word, anybody can be transformed and redeemed. That's the real truth — and the stakes are daily; one day of bad thinking can produce decisions driven by fear, anxiety, pride, or insecurity.

A Word Rich Enough for All of Life

The New Testament alone contains a thousand commands for how to live: on marriage and intimacy, alcohol, what a minister should be (1 and 2 Timothy), envy, pride. Whatever your gift — teaching, healing, prayer — using the Word to strengthen you is never a bad idea.

Put On the Full Armor of God

"Be strong in the Lord and in his mighty power. Put on the full armor of God, so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes" (Ephesians 6:10-11). Why armor? Because "our struggle is not against flesh and blood," but against the spiritual forces of evil (Ephesians 6:12). Belt of truth, breastplate of righteousness, shield of faith to extinguish the flaming arrows of the evil one, helmet of salvation — all culminating in one weapon: "the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God" (Ephesians 6:17).

When God's Instruction Contradicts Common Sense

The biggest decisions of your life — whom to marry, where to move — are a God thing; no amount of scientific PhDs can settle them. And his instruction can often contradict:

  • Your common sense.
  • Your own desires.
  • What your friends think and say.
  • Even what your parents say.

But here is the amazing thing: God's instruction will never contradict God's Word — his truth is eternal, unique, and unchanging. Studying it strengthens your intuition; real discernment always aligns with that truth.

The Lord Our God Is One

At the center of everything is Deuteronomy 6:4, the Shema, the foundational declaration of Judaism: "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one." When experts in the law, hoping to trap him, asked Jesus which of six-hundred-plus laws was most important, he opened with that very sentence — then: love God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and "love your neighbor as yourself" (Mark 12:29-31). Love God, love people.

The Hebrew word for "one" is echad — not a singular oneness like one phone, but a oneness with complexity. The same word appears when a man is united to his wife "and they become one flesh" (Genesis 2:24). The oneness of husband and wife is the oneness of God: more oneness in families means more of God in the world. "Whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven" (Matthew 16:19). The goal is unbreakable faith and unbreakable families — and unbreakable faith is for every single person, married or not.

Life Application

This week, be a Berean: examine the Scriptures every day. Choose one passage each morning — start with Ephesians 6:10-18 — and read it not as homework but as armor. When a lie shows up ("I can't change," "they'll never change"), answer it out loud with the Word, the way Jesus did in the wilderness.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • When did your heart last burn within you over Scripture — and what stirred it?
  • Which of the enemy's lies, about yourself or someone else, have you been believing lately?
  • Where is God's instruction contradicting your common sense, desires, or the voices around you?
  • What would pursuing oneness — with God and in your closest relationships — look like this week?

Open the Word with eagerness, carry the fire, and let it power you up to be greater, do more, and love better. After all, if it's good enough for Jesus, it's good enough for me — and it's good enough for you.
